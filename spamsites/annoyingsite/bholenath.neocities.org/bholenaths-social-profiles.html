Some Additional Thoughts on Social Media

I'm still not big on social media, and I can give only the (personal) opinions that I'm making about them as I go.

My social media profiles are still kind of underdeveloped, and the not fully formed opinions I can give about the social media sites are only partially useful.

The communities present on the social media sites are what's more important about them. It's difficult to give an accurate opinion on social media sites, because the experience is always subjective and one-sided, based on the perceptions of the person voicing that opinion.

Many reasons can influence your usage of social media for horror, a few needs that come to mind:

  1. To Belong to The Horror Crowd at Large
  2. To do Horror Business Networking
  3. To Maintain the Social Media Presence of a Horror-related Business
  4. To Join Horror Communities
  5. To add more Horror People to your Contacts
  6. To keep up to date with Horror News
  7. To do Horror Social Networking
  8. To enjoy Horror Entertainment
  9. To be Alerted of Upcoming Horror Events
  10. To find a Horror-related job
  11. To have a Horror fans Support System
  12. To share Horror-related Stuff

Whatever your motivation to join a social networking site as a fan of the macabre, my best suggestion is this: study and additionally join all of those that attract you, as your available time permits.

I'm offering you this guidance since you can never realize beforehand which site will be more pleasing to you if you don't join most of them and connect with horror fans in them all.

Until you use them (even prior to joining, while you're lurking and considering joining), you won't know how the horror groups on each site function, and you won't have the option to try and make a guess of how it feels to actively participate, since you will not have hands-on experience.

Additionally, it's essential to join and utilize the most that you can, since you will not know in which of them you will feel more comfortable until you join and interact with others like you. That is the reason it's smarter to join the most that you would be able and use them for some time (say, a half year) until choosing to just zero in on one or a few social media sites.
